Iquitos Peru

Iquitos is the largest city in the Peruvian Amazon and is often called the “capital of the Amazon.” It is unique because it is one of the world's largest cities that cannot be reached by road; instead, visitors must arrive by boat or plane. Surrounded by rivers and lush rainforest, Iquitos is a gateway to exploring the Amazon's incredible biodiversity. The city itself is lively, with a mix of Indigenous, Spanish, and modern influences, and it is known for its floating neighborhood of Belén, colorful markets, and eco-tourism.
